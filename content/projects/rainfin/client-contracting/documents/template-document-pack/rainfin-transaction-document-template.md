## 5. Detailed Fee Calculation Examples

### 5.1 **Funding Transaction Example**

5.1.1 Example: A Customer funds their wallet with R 500 and the fee is R 15, calculated at 3%. After the fee, the wallet is funded with R 485. In this example, R 500 is processed, R 15 is allocated as fees, and R 485 is available to the Customer.

### 5.2 **Cash Out Transaction Example**

5.2.1 Example: A Customer cashes out R 200 and the fee is R 3 fixed. The Customer receives R 197 in their bank account. In this example, R 200 is processed, R 3 is allocated as fees, and R 197 is cashed out to the Customer.

### 5.3 **Payment Transaction Example**

5.3.1 Example: A Customer pays R 200 for goods or services and the transaction fee is R 2 fixed. The Customer pays a total of R 202. In this example, R 200 is paid to the merchant or retailer and R 2 is allocated as fees.

### 5.4 **Withdrawal Calculation Examples**

5.4.1 Example 1: A withdrawal of R 100 that attracts a fee of R 3 will result in R 103 being deducted from the Customer's wallet.

5.4.2 Example 2: A Customer that only has R 100 in their wallet and wants to withdraw R 100 may not be able to do so if the applicable fee must also be covered from the wallet balance.

5.4.3 Example 3: A payment to a retailer or till point may have the fee added to the purchase or transaction amount. A payment of R 300 with a fee of R 2 may result in R 302 being deducted from the Customer's wallet.
