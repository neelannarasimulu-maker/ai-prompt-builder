## Visible Text

Title: Customer Success And KPI System
Body: Customer Success is the operating office for adoption, value tracking and expansion discipline.
Body: It defines what success means, tracks whether the programme is moving, and turns programme evidence into investor-visible proof.

Section: Phase Delivery Approach
Item: Phase 1: controlled readiness, pilot onboarding and operating cadence
Item: Phase 2: staff and member activation at scale
Item: Phase 3: public launch, retailer payment growth and rewards expansion
Item: Phase 4: optimisation, repeat usage, AUM growth and broader programme expansion

Section: KPI Definitions
Metric: Registrations: members registered for the wallet
Metric: Activation rate: registered members completing a first meaningful wallet action
Metric: Active users: members using wallet, token, payment, reward or savings capability
Metric: Repeat usage: members returning after first action
Metric: AUM: customer balances participating in the money-market-backed value proposition
Metric: Redemption activity: voucher issue, usage and direct POS redemption
Metric: Support quality: support volumes, resolution time and issue closure

Section: Target Management
Action: Define registration and activation targets by launch phase
Action: Define value and volume targets for wallet usage
Action: Define AUM targets for growth-earning balances
Action: Define retailer payment and voucher redemption targets
Action: Track weekly movement against agreed programme KPIs
Action: Convert KPI evidence into expansion and investor proof

Section: Executive Cadence
Item: Advisory Forum rhythm
Item: Programme issue ownership
Item: Launch and adoption reporting
Item: Commercial follow-through
Item: Expansion planning

## Image Brief

Create a customer-success operating-office slide. Use phase gates, KPI cards and target-management lanes. Show Customer Success as the discipline that links programme delivery, adoption metrics, AUM targets, rewards usage, support quality and expansion planning. Avoid generic CRM visuals. Make it feel like an investor-grade programme control room.
