# BLOCK MARKETS AFRICA / OPEN

# Indicative Pricing Framework

**Generic Client Template**

# 1. Purpose of this Indicative Pricing Framework

This document provides a high-level indicative pricing framework to help a prospective client assess the potential cost of onboarding onto the Block Markets Africa / Open platform.

The purpose of this document is to support early business case evaluation only. It is not a final commercial proposal, rate card, contract, mandate, or binding offer.

# 2. Key Pricing Assumptions

The indicative pricing framework is based on the following assumptions:

1. The prospective client will contract with Block Markets Africa / Open as the Client.
2. The Client’s members, users, employees, beneficiaries, customers, merchants, or programme participants will be treated as the Client’s customers.
3. The solution may include business wallets, customer wallets, payment transactions, wallet transactions, platform access, API and/or UI connectivity, KYC, reporting, reconciliation, and support services.
4. KYC services may include Sumsub or another approved KYC, AML, or verification provider, subject to the agreed onboarding model.
5. Third-party provider fees, including KYC, AML, payment processing, vouchers, messaging, bank charges, or other external costs, may be passed through separately or included in a bundled fee depending on the final commercial model.
6. Interest income or yield earned on qualifying wallet balances, where applicable, will be subject to the approved regulatory, treasury, and commercial structure.
7. Any Juristic Representative arrangement, where required, will be scoped separately and may affect commercial terms, oversight requirements, reporting obligations, compliance monitoring, and yield distribution.
8. Final pricing remains subject to business case review, compliance review, risk review, solution design, commercial approval, and contract finalisation.

# 3. Indicative Pricing Components

The final commercial model may include one or more of the following pricing components.

# 4. Commercial Qualification

All pricing remains indicative until the business case has been reviewed and the commercial model has been approved.

The final commercial proposal may include a combination of setup fees, monthly platform fees, KYC fees, JR fees, transaction fees, service fees, support fees, third-party pass-through costs, and any agreed treatment of interest income or yield.

No pricing, fee, yield allocation, or commercial structure should be regarded as final or binding until confirmed in an approved commercial proposal and signed agreement.
