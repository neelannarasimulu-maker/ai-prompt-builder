# 8. South African Market Reference Summary

South Africa is a structurally import-dependent coffee market. Domestic production exists, but it is small relative to commercial roasting, hospitality, café, office and retail demand.

The key market research finding is that South Africa imports significantly more green coffee than roasted coffee. This indicates that South African roasters, brands and manufacturers prefer importing green coffee and adding value locally through roasting, blending, packaging and distribution.

The strongest initial opportunity is therefore not finished retail coffee import. It is green coffee supply into South African roasters and coffee businesses.

# 15. Import And Regulatory Reference Notes

| Topic | Working Reference |
|---|---|
| Main import category | Green coffee, HS 090111 |
| Customs duty | Green coffee is generally listed as free of customs duty in SARS tariff schedules, subject to confirmation of HS classification |
| Import VAT | VAT cash flow must be planned even where duty is free |
| Green coffee regulation | Green coffee for further roasting is treated differently from retail-ready coffee products |
| Documents | Certificate of origin, phytosanitary certificate, commercial invoice, packing list and transport document are critical |
| Clearing | A South African clearing agent should review documents before shipment |
| Strategy principle | Do not ship before documents, buyer demand and landed cost are validated |
