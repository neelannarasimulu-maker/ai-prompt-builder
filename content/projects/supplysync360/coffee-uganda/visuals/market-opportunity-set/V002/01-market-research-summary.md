### Coffee Import Opportunity: Market Research Summary

South Africa is structurally dependent on imported coffee, with local production remaining small relative to national consumption.

The import market is weighted heavily toward green coffee rather than finished roasted products.

In 2024, South Africa imported approximately 22.7 million kg of non-decaffeinated green coffee valued at about US$98.4 million.

The strongest opportunity is therefore not to launch a retail coffee brand first, but to import green beans and supply local roasters, cafés, hospitality suppliers and coffee brands.

Uganda and Ethiopia are both already present in South African green coffee imports.

### Research Conclusion

A coffee import and distribution model is viable if SupplySync360 validates demand first, starts with green coffee, and builds a phased East African sourcing portfolio.
