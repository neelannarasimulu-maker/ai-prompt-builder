# South African Coffee Market Overview

## Consumption Growth

South Africa remains one of the largest coffee-consuming markets in Africa.

Over the past two decades, coffee consumption has grown steadily due to:

- Urbanisation
- Rising disposable income
- Growth of café culture
- Expansion of premium food and beverage markets
- Increased workplace coffee consumption

Coffee has evolved from a discretionary luxury purchase into a mainstream consumer product.

---

## Structural Market Shift

Historically, tea dominated hot beverage consumption in South Africa.

This dynamic has changed significantly.

Younger consumers increasingly prefer coffee.

Urban consumers increasingly consume coffee outside the home.

Premium coffee consumption continues to rise.

This creates sustained demand for imported coffee.

---

# South African Import Dependency

## Domestic Production Versus Consumption

South Africa produces coffee domestically in limited quantities.

Production is concentrated primarily in:

- KwaZulu-Natal
- Mpumalanga
- Limpopo

However, domestic production satisfies only a small proportion of total demand.

The industry therefore relies heavily on imported coffee.

---

## Strategic Significance

Import dependency creates a structural opportunity.

SupplySync360 is not attempting to create demand.

Demand already exists.

The objective is to participate within an established supply chain.

---

# Green Coffee Import Analysis

## Market Structure

South Africa imports substantially more green coffee than roasted coffee.

This is important because it confirms that value addition occurs domestically.

Imported green coffee is subsequently:

- Roasted
- Blended
- Packaged
- Distributed

This structure supports the recommended market-entry strategy.

---

## Strategic Implications

The opportunity lies primarily within:

Green Coffee

rather than

Finished Consumer Products

during the initial phases.

This reduces complexity while maximising market access.
