# Introduction

One of the most common causes of failure in agricultural import businesses is inadequate supplier due diligence.

Many first-time importers focus heavily on product quality and pricing while underestimating the importance of exporter capability, operational maturity, financial stability and supply-chain governance.

Coffee sourcing relationships frequently fail because of:

- Inconsistent quality
- Overstated production capacity
- Weak export capabilities
- Poor communication
- Documentation deficiencies
- Logistics failures
- Financial instability

Consequently, supplier selection should be treated as a strategic decision rather than a procurement activity.

The objective of this section is to establish a framework that enables SupplySync360 to evaluate potential Ugandan and Ethiopian partners systematically and reduce commercial risk before committing capital.

---

# Supplier Due Diligence Philosophy

## The Fundamental Question

The primary question should not be:

"Can this supplier provide coffee?"

The primary question should be:

"Can this supplier consistently support a growing commercial relationship over the next three to five years?"

This distinction is critical.

Many suppliers can successfully fulfil a single shipment.

Far fewer can support long-term growth.

The objective is therefore to identify strategic partners rather than transactional vendors.

---

# Corporate Due Diligence

## Legal Entity Verification

The first step in supplier assessment is confirming the legal existence of the business.

SupplySync360 should request:

- Company registration documents
- Business licence
- Tax registration certificates
- Export registration documentation

Verification should occur independently wherever possible.

---

## Ownership Structure

Understanding ownership is important for several reasons.

Ownership affects:

- Decision-making authority
- Governance quality
- Succession planning
- Commercial stability

Questions should include:

- Who owns the company?
- Who are the directors?
- Are there minority shareholders?
- Who signs contracts?
- Who controls export operations?

---

## Business History

Supplier maturity is often a useful predictor of future performance.

Key questions include:

- How long has the business operated?
- How long has it exported coffee?
- Which countries does it currently serve?
- Has it previously supplied South Africa?

The objective is not necessarily to exclude younger businesses.

Rather, it is to understand experience levels and associated risks.

---

# Financial Due Diligence

## Financial Stability

Coffee exports require working capital.

Exporters must often purchase coffee, process product and arrange logistics before receiving payment.

Weak financial positions can create operational risks.

Questions should include:

- How are purchases financed?
- Does the company utilise trade finance?
- Has the company experienced cash-flow challenges?
- Can production be scaled if demand increases?

---

## Banking Verification

SupplySync360 should request:

- Banking details
- Account verification
- Export payment arrangements

This reduces fraud risk and improves transaction confidence.

---

# Export Capability Assessment

## Export History

One of the strongest indicators of future performance is successful export history.

Questions should include:

- Export destinations
- Export volumes
- Customer references
- Export frequency

Supporting evidence should be requested wherever possible.

---

## Export Documentation Competence

The supplier should demonstrate familiarity with:

- Commercial invoices
- Packing lists
- Bills of lading
- Certificates of origin
- Phytosanitary certificates
- Export permits

Weaknesses in this area often lead to shipment delays.

---

# Production Capacity Assessment

## Volume Verification

Many suppliers overestimate available production capacity.

SupplySync360 should seek clarity regarding:

- Annual production capacity
- Available export volumes
- Existing customer commitments
- Expansion potential

The objective is to determine realistic supply capability.

---

## Scalability

An exporter may be capable of supplying 250 kilograms but unable to support 20 tons.

Growth potential should therefore be evaluated separately from current capability.

---

# Quality Assurance Assessment

## Quality Systems

Coffee quality should never be assumed.

Questions should include:

- How is quality measured?
- What grading systems are used?
- What rejection rates occur?
- How are defects managed?

---

## Cupping Procedures

Professional coffee suppliers should understand cupping.

Questions include:

- Are coffees cupped before export?
- Who performs evaluations?
- What quality standards are applied?

---

## Consistency Controls

Consistency is often more valuable than occasional excellence.

Buyers require repeatability.

SupplySync360 should evaluate:

- Batch controls
- Processing controls
- Storage conditions
- Quality monitoring systems

# Red Flags

Several indicators should trigger caution.

## Commercial Red Flags

- Unusually low prices
- Pressure for immediate payment
- Reluctance to provide samples
- Inability to explain quality processes

---

## Operational Red Flags

- No export history
- Incomplete documentation
- Inconsistent responses
- Poor communication

---

## Governance Red Flags

- Unclear ownership
- Lack of contracts
- Unverifiable business information
