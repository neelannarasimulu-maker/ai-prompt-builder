The next challenge is execution.

Execution requires customers.

The objective of this section is to identify:

- Which organisations should be targeted.

- Which customer segments should be prioritised.
- How conversations should be structured.
- How SupplySync360 should position itself.

The goal is to move from market research to revenue generation.

---

# Customer Acquisition Principles

## Focus On Buyers Before Importing

One of the largest mistakes made by new importers is purchasing inventory before validating customer demand.

SupplySync360 should reverse this process.

The recommended sequence is:

Identify Buyers

↓

Validate Interest

↓

Evaluate Samples

↓

Obtain Indicative Demand

↓

Import Pilot Volumes

This significantly reduces commercial risk.

# Strategic Assessment

The South African market contains sufficient customer diversity to support a phased market-entry strategy.

The strongest immediate opportunities are likely to emerge from:

- Commercial roasters
- Specialty roasters
- Independent coffee brands

These segments provide the optimal balance between:

- Accessibility
- Volume
- Margin potential
- Customer-development effort

The evidence suggests that customer acquisition should occur before meaningful inventory commitments are made.

This reduces risk, improves capital efficiency and increases the probability of a successful pilot programme.

The ultimate objective should be to establish SupplySync360 as the preferred East African sourcing partner for South African coffee buyers while simultaneously laying the foundation for a broader East African trade and supply-chain ecosystem.
