# Strategic Objective

The objective during the first 100 days is not to build a large coffee business.

The objective is to answer six critical questions.

---

## Question 1

Can the suppliers consistently provide the required coffee?

---

## Question 2

Does the quality meet South African market expectations?

---

## Question 3

Will South African buyers purchase the product?

---

## Question 4

Can logistics be executed reliably?

---

## Question 5

Can acceptable margins be achieved?

---

## Question 6

Does the opportunity justify additional investment?

---

# 100-Day Success Definition

The programme should be considered successful if, within 100 days, SupplySync360 achieves:

- Validated suppliers
- Approved coffee samples
- Buyer interest
- Pilot customers
- Confirmed logistics pathway
- Positive commercial feedback

Revenue is desirable.

Validation is essential.

## Sample Evaluation

- Comparative evaluations

## Success Criteria

Positive feedback from multiple evaluators.

# Priority 3

## Buyer Discovery

Understand demand before importing.

Activities:

- Customer interviews
- Market validation
- Product discussions

## Success Criteria

At least ten meaningful customer conversations.

# Priority 4

## Pilot Demand Validation

Determine whether buyers will purchase.

Activities:

- Trial commitments
- Indicative volume discussions

## Success Criteria

Three to five credible pilot opportunities.

# Priority 5

- Freight quotations

- Customs assessment

## Success Criteria

# Executive Recommendation

The next 100 days should focus on validation rather than scale.

The strongest path forward is:

1. Validate Uganda.
2. Validate demand.
3. Execute pilot imports.
4. Expand through proven demand.
5. Introduce Ethiopia strategically.
6. Leverage SupplySync360 technology as the long-term differentiator.

The opportunity should be approached as a phased strategic initiative rather than a trading venture.

If executed successfully, coffee may become the foundation of a broader East African trade and supply-chain ecosystem capable of creating substantially greater long-term value than coffee alone.
