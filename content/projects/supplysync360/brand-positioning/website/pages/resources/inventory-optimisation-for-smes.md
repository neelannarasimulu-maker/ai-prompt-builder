# Inventory Optimisation for SMEs

A practical framework for SME teams to reduce stockouts and excess inventory without enterprise-scale overhead.

## In Simple Language

This guide helps small and medium teams hold the right stock at the right time, so cash is not trapped in excess inventory and shelves do not run empty.

## Guide Overview

Start with service-level segmentation: not all SKUs deserve the same stock policy. Prioritise high-impact items and define simple guardrails by product group.

Use weekly demand and supplier reliability reviews to tune reorder points and safety stock. Small, consistent adjustments outperform occasional large resets.

Connect replenishment decisions to real operational constraints including supplier lead-time volatility, storage limits, and last-mile delivery realities.

SMEs gain the fastest value when inventory decisions are embedded in one operational workflow instead of spread across disconnected sheets and tools.

## Assumptions

- At least six months of SKU movement history is available.
- Supplier lead-time patterns can be tracked for key suppliers.
- Teams review replenishment exceptions at least weekly.
- Product segmentation is defined by service criticality.

## How SupplySync360 Delivers These Outcomes

1. Brings inventory, supplier lead-time, and demand signals into one operational view.
2. Applies configurable replenishment rules by SKU group and service priority.
3. Flags expiry and stockout risk early so teams can rebalance before disruption occurs.
4. Supports weekly policy tuning with practical dashboards instead of manual spreadsheet reconciliation.
