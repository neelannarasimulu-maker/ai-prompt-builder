# Built for Multiple Markets. Ready for Any Scale.

From lean SME teams to regulated enterprise operations, deploy practical supply models that improve visibility, control, and execution speed.

## Solution Entry Points

Buyers should be able to find the right path in one scan.

Navigate by industry, operating model, or business size depending on how your team frames the problem.

### By Industry

Match the platform to sector-specific execution realities.

See how retail, FMCG, manufacturing, pharma, agriculture, and mixed-channel networks use differentiated orchestration models.

### By Operating Model

Start from the shape of the network you run.

Choose solution blueprints for supplier-to-store connectedness, cluster governance, last-mile orchestration, and mixed trade operations.

### By Business Size

Choose a rollout path that fits current maturity.

Find the right starting point for SME fast-starts, mid-market standardisation, and enterprise phased programs.

## Industry Coverage

Built for environments where service, control, and response speed compete every day.

| Industry | Positioning |
|---|---|
| Retail | Run a dual marketing model that serves modern trade key accounts and independent neighborhood stores with one coordinated supply engine. |
| FMCG | Coordinate brand-led demand creation and distributor-led route-to-market execution through a dual marketing FMCG operating model. |
| Manufacturing | Support a dual marketing manufacturing strategy by synchronising OEM/key-account fulfilment and aftermarket channel replenishment. |
| Pharma | Execute a dual healthcare marketing model that supports institutional tenders and retail pharmacy demand without compromising compliance. |
| Automotive | Enable a dual automotive go-to-market motion across OEM production demand and dealer/aftermarket service demand. |
| Music and Entertainment | Operate a dual marketing entertainment model that unifies B2B venue/event distribution and direct-to-fan retail merchandising. |

The same platform can start light or scale deep, depending on the operation.

| Path | Description | First-Value Window |
|---|---|---|

| Mid-Market Operators | Standardise planning and execution across sites, teams, and channels without locking into heavy legacy stacks. | 4-10 weeks |
| Enterprise Networks | Coordinate multi-entity operations with robust controls while maintaining the agility to execute quickly. | 8-16 weeks by rollout phase |
