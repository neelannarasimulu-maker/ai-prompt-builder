## Body Content

# Industries

Explore industry-specific solution paths designed for practical execution across SME, mid-market, and enterprise environments.

## Industry Strategy

Show buyers that the platform understands channel realities, not just generic supply workflows.

Each industry path should clarify the service model, risk pattern, and execution trade-offs that matter most in that environment.

- Retail and FMCG channel orchestration.
- Regulated and expiry-sensitive pharma workflows.
- Manufacturing, agriculture, and mixed-channel control models.

## What These Pages Should Answer

| Question | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Where does service risk build first? | Highlight the core pressure points for each sector before describing the response model. |
| How does the operating model differ by channel? | Explain the mix between national accounts, local outlets, institutions, or dealer networks. |
| What outcome should a buyer expect? | Tie each blueprint to service reliability, inventory posture, and governance quality. |

## Solutions By Industry

| Industry | Description | Primary Challenge |
|---|---|---|
| Retail | Run a dual marketing model that serves modern trade key accounts and independent neighborhood stores with one coordinated supply engine. | Retail teams must protect on-shelf availability for national chains while also supporting high-frequency, low-volume replenishment for fragmented local outlets. |
| FMCG | Coordinate brand-led demand creation and distributor-led route-to-market execution through a dual marketing FMCG operating model. | FMCG organisations need to align national brand campaigns with hyper-local sell-through realities across wholesalers, retail chains, and informal channels. |
| Manufacturing | Support a dual marketing manufacturing strategy by synchronising OEM/key-account fulfilment and aftermarket channel replenishment. | Manufacturers must protect contract commitments for large buyers while rapidly supplying diverse aftermarket partners with different order patterns and SLAs. |
| Pharma | Execute a dual healthcare marketing model that supports institutional tenders and retail pharmacy demand without compromising compliance. | Pharma operators need to deliver assured supply to hospitals and public programmes while maintaining high service levels for private pharmacies and clinics. |
| Automotive | Enable a dual automotive go-to-market motion across OEM production demand and dealer/aftermarket service demand. | Automotive networks face conflicting priorities between just-in-time plant supply and unpredictable dealer or service-part consumption. |
| Music and Entertainment | Operate a dual marketing entertainment model that unifies B2B venue/event distribution and direct-to-fan retail merchandising. | Entertainment brands must execute fixed event commitments while reacting quickly to volatile fan demand across online and physical merchandise channels. |
| Agriculture | Run a dual agriculture marketing strategy that serves large institutional buyers and fragmented agro-dealer networks from one system. | Agriculture supply chains must balance seasonal bulk commitments to enterprise buyers with frequent, localized replenishment to rural SME channels. |
