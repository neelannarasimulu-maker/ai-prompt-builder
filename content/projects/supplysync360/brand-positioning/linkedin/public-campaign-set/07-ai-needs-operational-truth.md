## LinkedIn Post Text

AI in supply chains is exciting. It is also easy to overstate.

A forecasting model cannot confidently recommend a purchase if it cannot see current stock across locations, delayed supplier orders, branch demand, expiry exposure, damaged returns, pending transfers or open exceptions.

That is where many AI conversations should begin: not with the model, but with the operating truth feeding the model.

For South African businesses, this matters because supply performance is shaped by practical volatility: supplier timing, transport disruption, branch-level demand, working-capital pressure and frontline execution gaps. A sales trend alone is not the whole truth.

The useful question is not “can AI predict?” It is “can the business see enough to let AI recommend the right action?”

SupplySync360 is built around that sequence:

1. Capture the operating signal.
2. Connect the signal to context.
3. Assign the next action.
4. Record evidence and closure.
5. Build AI-ready data from real execution.

That is how AI becomes practical: less theatre, more operating control.
