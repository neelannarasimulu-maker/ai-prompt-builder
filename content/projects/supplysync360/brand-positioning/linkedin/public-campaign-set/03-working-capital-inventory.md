## LinkedIn Post Text
Inventory in South Africa is a working-capital shock absorber

When logistics is unpredictable, many businesses buy more stock. The problem is that every buffer has a cash cost.

South African supply teams understand why buffers exist. Freight uncertainty, supplier delays, municipal disruptions and currency pressure all make “just enough” inventory feel risky.

But excess stock is not free resilience. It ties up cash, takes space, creates ageing risk and can still fail the customer if it sits in the wrong location.

The discipline is to know which buffer is protecting service and which buffer is quietly becoming waste.

SupplySync360 helps businesses see inventory across owned and linked locations, so teams can transfer, reorder, reserve or escalate from a shared operating picture.
