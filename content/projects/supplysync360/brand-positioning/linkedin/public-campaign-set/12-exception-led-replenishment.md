## LinkedIn Post Text
A reorder rule can be correct on paper and wrong in the real world.

If another branch has stock, the best action may be transfer.
If the supplier is late, the best action may be escalation.
If a batch is expiring, the best action may be discount or redistribution.
If cash is tight, the best action may be to reserve, substitute or wait.
If demand has shifted, the best action may be to adjust the policy.

That is why replenishment should start with exceptions, not just minimum and maximum quantities.

SupplySync360 connects the signals that shape the decision: stock on hand, linked-business availability, supplier orders, expiry exposure, branch requests, returns and action ownership.

The goal is practical control: protect service, reduce waste and avoid buying stock when a better move already exists inside the network.
